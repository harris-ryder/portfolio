Making masks for the NHS

Like many unprecidented events, the beginning of Covid-19 brought about a shortage in medical supplies. This included face masks for hospital staff. Reading about nurses having to wear bin bags because PPE was not available made me act.

I arranged a meeting with the head of my company, and was able to convince them to give me a small investment to procure 3D printers. With these printers the plan was to find and modify an existing face mask design and start a production line.

There were multiple 3D designs out there. I found 3DVerkstan´s design to be the most optimal, in terms of material use and output.

I spent days increasing output by trying different print settings. Such as print arrangements on the bed, trying to reduce unproductive nozzle movement. Thinning out the actual product design.

I also added custom GCODE to the printer so that the printer could automatically remove the print from the print bed and start again. See video. This update doubled output, with no human required, the printers ran all night.

We produced over 2000 masks, we sold 1500 to the NHS and donated 500+ to care homes.
